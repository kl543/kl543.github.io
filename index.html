About

I am a Physics PhD student at UIUC. My background is experimental: MBE growth of high-quality thin films and STM measurements (topography, dI/dV, and reliable tip conditioning). I also spent time at UC Berkeley doing STM, and later worked on computational modeling of Rydberg-atom dynamics.

My clear career goal is quantitative finance — specifically QR/QD/QT. I am actively retooling: this year I am taking ML and stochastic-process courses, and next term I plan to take CS 446 (Machine Learning), Optimization, Time Series, and finance/market-microstructure coursework. I am also transitioning to a more computation/ML-focused group and pursuing a STAT/CS minor to deepen the foundations. I enjoy building clean, reproducible pipelines and turning data into testable signals and cost-aware backtests. I am targeting Summer 2026 quant internships—and I’m confident I will get there. I’m also open to applied AI roles that strengthen modeling and engineering skills along the way.
